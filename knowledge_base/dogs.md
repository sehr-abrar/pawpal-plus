# Dog Care Guide

## Feeding Schedule
Adult dogs should be fed 2 times per day, roughly 12 hours apart. Puppies under 6 months need 3-4 meals daily. Senior dogs (7+ years) may do better on 2-3 smaller meals to aid digestion. Measure portions by weight and calorie guidelines on food packaging to avoid obesity. Always provide fresh water.

## Exercise Requirements
Dogs need daily physical activity to stay healthy and prevent behavioral problems.
- Small breeds (Chihuahua, Shih Tzu, Pug): 20-30 minutes per day
- Medium breeds (Beagle, Cocker Spaniel, Border Collie): 45-60 minutes per day
- Large breeds (Labrador, German Shepherd, Golden Retriever): 60-90 minutes per day
- High-energy working breeds (Husky, Malinois): 90-120 minutes per day
Walks should ideally be split into 2 sessions. Puppies follow the 5-minute rule: 5 minutes per month of age, twice a day.

## Medications and Preventatives
- Flea and tick prevention: monthly topical or oral treatment (e.g., Frontline, NexGard)
- Heartworm prevention: monthly oral tablet (e.g., Heartgard) year-round in most climates
- Deworming: every 3-6 months depending on lifestyle and risk
- Core vaccines: Rabies annually or every 3 years per local law; DHPP (distemper, hepatitis, parvovirus, parainfluenza) every 1-3 years after puppy series
- Non-core vaccines: Bordetella (kennel cough) annually if boarded or in dog parks

## Vet Visits
- Puppies: every 3-4 weeks from 6-16 weeks of age for vaccines and checkups
- Adults (1-7 years): annual wellness exam and bloodwork
- Seniors (7+ years): biannual exams to catch age-related conditions early

## Grooming
- Short-haired breeds: brush weekly, bathe every 4-6 weeks
- Long-haired breeds: brush daily to prevent matting, bathe every 4-6 weeks
- All dogs: trim nails every 3-4 weeks; brush teeth 2-3 times weekly; clean ears monthly

## Warning Signs Requiring Urgent Vet Attention
Loss of appetite for more than 24 hours, vomiting or diarrhea lasting more than 24 hours, difficulty breathing, pale gums, sudden lethargy, seizures, bloated abdomen, inability to urinate, or ingestion of toxic substances (chocolate, xylitol, grapes, raisins, onions, garlic).
